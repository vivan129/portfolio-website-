Human Size Robot

Click for more

I have built a human-sized autonomous robot that can navigate indoors and outdoors, talk to people, and perform practical tasks like fetching items, helping in the house, and even gardening. The robot runs on a dual-brain system, with a main brain handling high-level decision making, speech, and navigation algorithms, while a secondary brain using a Raspberry Pi processes sensor data and manages precise motor control. A LIDAR sensor gives it 360-degree mapping ability, allowing it to move safely, avoid obstacles, and adapt to new environments using SLAM. It is equipped with speech recognition and AI-powered voice synthesis, enabling natural conversations and polite interactions with strangers. For gardening, it can water plants, monitor soil conditions, and reduce human effort. Building it at the age of 14 required me to solve challenges like synchronizing multiple systems, managing stability, integrating voice with movement, and optimizing energy use. What makes it unique is not just its size but its ability to act as both a functional assistant and a social companion, showing how robotics can combine engineering, AI, and creativity to improve everyday life.
